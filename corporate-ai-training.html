Discuss my needs

Why train your teams in AI now?

Corporate AI training has moved from optional to a competitive necessity: according to Bpifrance Le Lab, 55% of French SMEs were using generative AI by the end of 2025 — up from 31% a year earlier — and the curve is similar across European markets and the Gulf. The catch: most of that usage is individual and unframed. Your staff already use ChatGPT, but without method, without a confidentiality framework and without structured gains for the company. Training turns that wild usage into an organised advantage.

What makes this different from a MOOC or a keynote?

Three things: your use cases, your tools, your data. Workshops run on participants' real tasks — answering a tender, preparing an annual review, analysing a client file — not on generic examples. Every session alternates demonstration, individual practice and the construction of a deliverable that is reusable the next morning. That is the difference between knowing AI exists and using it every day.

I also teach these same subjects to students at 7 higher education institutions (TBS Education, YNOV, Purpan, Digital Campus…), which forces the content to stay at the state of the art: AI agents, RAG, MCP, low-code platforms. For technical teams that want to go beyond augmented office work, the programme can include Claude Code, agent design and advanced automation — in line with the AI architecture & solutions offer.

How much does corporate AI training cost?

The fee depends on the format (1 to 5 days), the number of participants and how customised the workshops are; it is quoted after the initial conversation, which is free. For French entities, the Qualiopi-certified framework (through training partner AS'COM) makes the programme eligible for OPCO funding, which frequently covers a significant share of the cost. International clients work on a direct engagement basis, with the same materials and method.

What happens after the training?

An isolated training evaporates within weeks if nothing follows. That is why every track ends with an anchoring plan: an internal AI champion identified, a shared prompt library, a usage policy, and follow-up points at 30 and 90 days. If the training reveals deeper automation needs, the AI diagnostic takes over to quantify and prioritize them.

Frequently asked questions

In which languages is the training delivered?

In English or French, on-site or remotely. Current engagements cover France, Dubai (UAE) and Flanders (Belgium). All materials, prompt libraries and follow-up documents are provided in the language of the session.

Which tools does the training cover?

The tools your teams will actually use: ChatGPT, Claude (including Claude Code and Cowork for technical profiles), Gemini, Perplexity, Mistral, and low-code automation platforms such as Make. The programme is adapted to your stack and your business lines.

How long does a corporate AI training take?

From 1 day (executive awareness) to 5 days (full operational track per business line, practising on your own use cases). In-company format is preferred: your teams, your data, your processes.

Do participants need technical skills?

No. Tracks are designed by profile: executives, managers, support functions, sales, technical teams. Everyone works on their own business use cases, with no programming prerequisites.
