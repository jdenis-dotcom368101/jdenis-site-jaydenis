What is an AI diagnostic for a business?

An AI diagnostic is a structured analysis of your business processes that identifies where artificial intelligence can produce a measurable gain: time saved, errors avoided, additional revenue. It delivers a prioritized, costed and compliant roadmap before any technical investment.

The context makes it urgent: according to Bpifrance Le Lab, 55% of French SMEs were using generative AI by the end of 2025, up from 31% a year earlier — and European markets follow the same curve. Yet most of this usage remains individual and unstructured: without a diagnostic, companies pile up subscriptions with no strategy, no data governance and no measured return on investment.

Who is this audit for — and who is it not for?

This service is designed for SMEs and mid-size companies across sectors — industry, construction, services, retail, regulated professions. I work from France with clients internationally: current engagements cover France, Dubai (UAE) and Flanders (Belgium), on-site or remote, in English or French.

What is the audit of existing AI solutions?

More and more companies have already invested: a customer chatbot, an "AI" add-on sold by a software vendor, an agency quote for an autonomous agent. The AI solution audit evaluates these tools or offers independently, on five axes:

  • Business relevance — does the tool solve a real problem, with a measurable gain?
  • Technical soundness — models used (GPT, Claude, Gemini, Mistral), architecture (RAG, agents, fine-tuning), dependencies and reversibility.
  • Total cost — licences, API consumption, maintenance, hidden data-cleaning costs.
  • Security and confidentiality — where your data goes, who processes it, under which contractual guarantees.
  • Compliance — EU AI Act classification of the system, GDPR obligations, required documentation.

The debrief gives you a reasoned verdict: keep, renegotiate, replace or stop.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How much does an AI diagnostic cost for an SME?

The initial assessment is free and without obligation: a one-hour interview to map your processes and identify the first use cases. The full audit is then quoted as a fixed fee depending on company size and the number of processes analysed — typically 2 to 5 days of work, on-site or remote.

What is the difference between an AI diagnostic and an AI solution audit?

The AI diagnostic assesses your organisation's maturity and identifies high-ROI use cases before any investment. The AI solution audit examines tools already in place or proposed by vendors: technical soundness, real costs, data security, EU AI Act and GDPR compliance.

How long does an AI audit take?

For an SME, expect 2 to 5 days spread over 2 to 4 weeks: business interviews, process and data analysis, and a debrief with a prioritized roadmap. Larger or multi-site organisations require specific scoping.

Do we need to already use AI to run a diagnostic?

No. The diagnostic is precisely the step that comes before the investment: it starts from your business processes, not from the tools. Many of the companies I advise had no structured AI usage at the start.
